### Adobe Photoshop Express: A Powerful Yet Free Photo Editor
Adobe Photoshop Express is a powerful yet free mobile photo editing app that packs cutting-edge features to improve your photos. Some of its key capabilities include adjusting color, brightness, and sharpness with sliders. While some more advanced tools require an Adobe Creative Cloud membership, the basic features make it an excellent full-featured option for free. Whether you need to lighten shadows, saturate colors, or sharpen details, Photoshop Express delivers pro-level adjustments without the cost.

### Snapseed Brings Non-Destructive Editing and Selective Adjustments 
Snapseed sets itself apart with non-destructive editing, allowing you to try out different filters and adjustments without permanently altering your original photos. Its selective editing brushes also target adjustments precisely to specific areas. Presets like Vintage expertly capture nostalgic looks with just a single tap. You can even download and edit RAW files directly within the app for maximum control over your photos. Snapseed remains one of the best all-around photo editors for both casual use and serious post-processing.

### Lightroom Pioneered the Camera Roll Editing Experience
Adobe Lightroom was one of the original apps to pioneer the camera roll approach to mobile photo editing. Its intuitive interface allows you to quickly swipe between your photos, flag your favorite shots, and delete any misses. Exposure, contrast, highlights, shadows, and other image parameters are all adjustable with sliders for granular control. Built-in presets and filters apply different looks to your photos in an instant. You can even export your edited shots as high-resolution JPEGs ready to share or print. Lightroom excels at quickly editing and organizing large batches of photos on the go.

### Polarr Caters to Creatives With Advanced Tools
Beyond basic adjustments for light, color, and warmth, Polarr offers more advanced tools ideal for portraits, landscapes, and other genres. Heal skin blemishes or straighten tilted horizons with just a tap. Over 30 built-in filters emulate different film styles, from grainy 127 film to faded Polaroids. Where Polarr truly shines is its thoughtfully designed interface clearly laid out for studio-quality edits. Advanced sliders and controls put serious retouching power in your hands whether you're an amateur or professional.

### PicsArt Empowers User Creativity With Collages, Filters, and More
As one of the most full-featured photo editors on mobile, PicsArt packs a surprisingly powerful set of tools while maintaining an approachable interface. Design impressive collages directly within the app or remove backgrounds from images in seconds using advanced selection tools. Filters range from nostalgic vintage effects to futuristic glitch textures. You can also draw, add creative stickers, crop to custom sizes, and more. ### Afterlight Focuses on Film Presets and Refinement Tools  
Afterlight strikes a balance as a photo editor tailored for both casual use and precision tweaks. Presets expertly emulate popular film stocks to transform images with authentic analog flair. Adjustment layers also allow non-destructively tweaking specific tonal ranges like shadows, highlights, or colors. Where Afterlight truly shines compared to competitors though are its sliders for fine-tuning light, clarity, textures and other subtle image parameters. Whether recreating nostalgic films or subtly polishing smartphone photos, Afterlight delivers.

### VSCO Inspires Creativity With Presets From Pro Photographers
VSCO stands for Visual Supply Company and focuses on presets crafted by award-winning photographers to inspire your creativity. Pre-made presets transform the entire look and feel of photos with a single tap. You can also start from scratch and finely tune exposures, colors, and styles on your own. Community-shared presets allow discovering new looks from other photographers as well. Cross-processing simulations expertly blend colors for dreamy analog-inspired edits ideal for portraiture and landscapes. VSCO remains a top choice for those seeking artistic inspiration above technical controls.

### Canva Goes Beyond Photos Into Graphics and Layouts  
While many editors focus only on photos, Canva takes a more well-rounded approach to visual content creation. Its library includes not only millions of stock photos but also icons, illustrations, graphics, and customizable text elements. Advanced templates handle multipage story layouts, resumes, social graphics and more. With Canva you can design virtually any visual asset. Precise alignment tools and layers put Pixel-perfect comps within reach. Whether you need a simple social graphic or elaborate digital magazine, Canva proves itself a invaluable mobile design studio.

### Fotor Brings Desktop-Level Tools to Your Smartphone
As one of the most full-featured photo editors on mobile, Fotor rivals what you'd find on desktop. Cut and refine selections, blend separate exposures, and remove redeye - all with a few intuitive taps. Where Fotor truly stands out is its layered approach, allowing combining adjustments, filters, frames and overlays into sophisticated compositions. Effects realistically alter specific image areas with sliders instead of one-size-fits-all filters. Whether you need basic touch-ups or advanced compositing, Fotor equips mobile photographers and designers with virtually desktop-class image editing on the go.
