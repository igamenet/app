## 2000+ Word Blog Post

### Note-Taking Apps

Microsoft OneNote is one of the most versatile note-taking apps available. It allows you to take notes in different "notebooks" and "sections" organized how you want. You can add images, videos, and files. Evernote is also great for organizing notes into different notebooks. You can search and retrieve your notes easily. Notion is a more advanced note-taking app that allows you to create databases, kanban boards and more. It has a very flexible format and is great for organizing larger projects and information. 


![](https://team-touchdroid.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/best-study-apps.png)
### Flashcard Apps

Anki is one of the most powerful flashcard apps for effective memorization. It uses spaced repetition to help you remember information for the long run. You can create digital flashcards for any subject. Anki uses algorithmic spacing to show you cards just before you're likely to forget them, which strengthens your memory. Quizlet is also very popular for creating and studying with flashcards. It offers learning tools like games to help engage content in a fun way.

### PDF Reader and Editor 

Adobe Acrobat Reader is the best free PDF reader for academics. It allows you to easily read, annotate, and fill out PDF documents. You can add notes, highlights and more directly into PDF files. For editing PDFs, many students love UPDF. It has more advanced editing tools like being able to add and move text in PDF documents. This is useful for making notes directly in PDF textbooks or assignments. 

### Time Management Apps

Google Calendar is one of the best free time management apps. It syncs across all your devices so you always know your schedule. You can color code classes, meetings, and more. Google Calendar also integrates well with Gmail and other Google services. Todoist is also great for tracking tasks and projects with reminders and due dates. The Focus Booster app helps you stay focused with the Pomodoro Technique, tracking your focus and break times to be more productive when studying.

### Language Learning Apps

Duolingo is one of the most popular language learning apps, making it fun and game-like to learn vocab and grammar. It has bite-sized lessons for many languages. Memrise takes a more memorization-focused approach with spaced repetition. The courses provide transcripts, audio, and images to Hammer new vocabulary into your long-term memory through memorization. Both apps are great for learning languages on a schedule no matter where you are.

### Math and Science Apps

Photomath allows you to simply take a photo of a math problem (handwritten or typed) and it will show you the step-by-step work and solution. This can be a real time-saver for homework and studying. Khan Academy offers free video lessons for everything from basic math to finance, physics, chemistry, biology and more. You can also practice problems and track your progress. Its challenges are also adaptive based on your strengths and weaknesses. 

### Coding Apps

SoloLearn is a great intro app for learning popular coding languages like Python, Java, and C# through fun mini-projects and interactive coding challenges. You can track your progress to keep motivated. Codecademy is also excellent with well-structured tutorials and interactive coding exercises in the browser. Both apps give you hands-on practice of the syntax, and exposure to building simple programs and algorithms in different languages.

### Mind Mapping Apps 

SimpleMind is a basic but effective free mind mapping app for visualizing information in a web of connected ideas. You can brainstorm, outlined and structure projects or topics of any kind in an intuitive way. MindMeister is a more full-featured paid mind mapping program that allows mind maps to be shared and collaborated on online in real-time. It also has extras like knowledge databases, Gantt charts and more advanced mapping tools. Both apps help turn abstract concepts into concrete visual webs.

### Exam Preparation Apps

Quizlet offers built-in study modes like flashcards, matching games and tests to help study large amounts of information for an upcoming exam. You can access premium study sets covering many subjects made by other Quizlet users. Khan Academy also has practice exams and question sets for major standardized tests like the SAT and MCAT as well as review videos and drills. Gradestack takes a more course-specific approach helping you prepare assignments, projects and exams based on your actual class schedules. It syncs to your Google Calendar.
