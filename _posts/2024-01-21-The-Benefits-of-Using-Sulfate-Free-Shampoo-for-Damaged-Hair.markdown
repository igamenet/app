### What are sulfates and why are they damaging
Sulfates are harsh surfactants commonly found in traditional shampoos. The most common type is sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS). While sulfates cleanse hair effectively by breaking down oils and dirt, the process is quite abrasive. Over time, the repeated stripping of hair's natural oils and protective layers leads to damage. Hair becomes dry, brittle and prone to breakage. Color treated or permed hair is especially vulnerable to the drying effects of sulfates.  


![](https://www.hairromance.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/Hair-Romance-How-to-uses-sulfate-free-shampoo.jpg)
### How sulfate-free shampoos are gentler   
Sulfate-free shampoos cleanse hair using milder surfactants that don't strip the scalp and hair shaft. These alternative surfactants break down dirt and oil without completely removing all natural oils. They help preserve hair's moisture balance and natural pH. The mild cleansing makes sulfate-free shampoos much gentler for damaged, color treated or chemically processed hair.

### Reducing frizz and flyaways
One of the main issues caused by sulfates is frizz. When natural oils are stripped away, hair cuticles remain raised and damaged. This causes hair to frizz easily in humid air. Sulfate-free shampoos help hair cuticles lay flat for a sleek, frizz-free look. They nourish hair to reduce drying and retain moisture so hair stays smooth and silky.

### Retaining hair color vibrancy    
Over time and repeated use, sulfates can begin to fade hair dye and permanent hair colors. The harsh surfactants disrupt hair's protective outer layer, making color molecules more vulnerable to being washed away. opting for sulfate-free shampoo preserves vibrant color for longer between dye jobs or touch-ups. Color treated hair stays bright and looks freshly dyed.

Some highly rated sulfate-free brands suitable for parched, damaged hair include DevaCurl One Condition, Klorane Gentle Shampoo with Licorice Extract and Not Your Mother's Naturals Blue Sea Kale & Coconut Water. These contain reparative conditioning agents like coconut oil, sea kale and licorice root extract to hydrate without weighing hair down. Brazilian blowout smoothing shampoos and color-protect shampoos from Ouidad and Joico are also top choices. 

### Choosing the right sulfate-free shampoo   
There is no one-size-fits-all sulfate-free shampoo. Consider your hair type such as dry, oily or color treated before choosing. Products with multiple conditioning plant-based surfactants like sugar syrups and coconut amino acids are best for rehabilitation. Some control oil and volume better than others. Read reviews to find the formula suited to your unique hair needs. A dermatologist recommendation can also help match hair concerns to the right product.

### Regular use for healthy hair
Switching to a gentle, reparative sulfate-free shampoo regimen and sticking to it is key for damaged hair recovery. Complement shampoo with occasional hair masks containing natural oils and proteins. Over time with consistent use, previously limp, brittle or dry hair becomes noticeably softer, shinier and fuller of body. Healthy hair grows in to replace damaged lengths for an overall look of well-nourished strength.
